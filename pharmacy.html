Era's College of Pharmacy

Era's College of Pharmacy nurtures skilled, ethical and innovative professionals to excel in pharmaceutical sciences and serve society./p>

click here to know more

Empowering healthcare through skill and service.

Era's College of Pharmacy, a proud constituent of Era University, is committed to shaping competent and compassionate pharmacy professionals. With state-of-the-art laboratories, research-driven learning and guidance from highly qualified faculty, the college blends academic rigor with practical exposure. It fosters innovation, ethical practice and a deep commitment to public health, preparing students to excel in pharmaceutical sciences and contribute meaningfully to society.

Diploma Programs

Gain professional healthcare skills with our diploma programs for a fast-track career.

D. Pharm

The Diploma in Pharmacy (D. Pharm) is a 2-year program that equips students with foundational knowledge and practical skills in pharmaceutical sciences, preparing them for roles in community and hospital pharmacies.
Duration: 2 years.

Bachelor's Programs

Shape your future in healthcare through 3 to 4 years of specialized study.

B. Pharm

The Bachelor of Pharmacy (B. Pharm) is a 4-year undergraduate program focused on pharmaceutical sciences, drug formulation and patient care. Graduates are prepared for careers in pharmacy practice, research and the pharmaceutical industry.
Duration: 4 years.

Master's Programs

Deepen your knowledge and skills through 2 years of focused study.

M. Pharm

The Master of Pharmacy (M. Pharm) is a 2-year postgraduate program focused on advanced pharmaceutical sciences, research, and industry-oriented skills. Graduates gain expertise for careers in research, academia, and the pharmaceutical sector.
Duration: 2 years.

Doctoral Programs

Deepen your knowledge and skills through 2 years of focused study.

Ph.D. in Pharmaceutical Sciences

The Ph.D. in Pharmaceutical Sciences is a research-intensive doctoral program that prepares scholars for advanced careers in academia, industry, and scientific innovation. Students conduct original research to advance knowledge in drug development, pharmacology, and related fields.
Duration: Minimum 3 years.
